Glossary

A

absolute poverty

is poverty so severe that one lacks resources to survive.

activity theory

states that life satisfaction depends on maintaining an involvement with life by developing new interests, hobbies, roles and relationships.

adolescence

is a physiological and psychological life stage used by psychologists to denote the period between puberty and adulthood.

age cohort

categorises people based on the year of their birth.

age structuring

refers to the way in which age is linked to a society's social structures, as are the roles and status of individuals within society.

ageism

is prejudice and discrimination based solely on age.

agnosticism

is the view that the truths about claims made by religions (such as the existence of an afterlife or deities) are unknowable.

alter-globalisation

is an alternative approach to globalisation that believes the positive benefits of globalisation cannot be achieved within existing neo-liberal economic structures.

alternative social movements

want to create a change in specific people's thoughts, practices and beliefs regarding a particular issue.

altruistic suicides

are suicides that occur when the level of solidarity is exceptionally high and when the individual views the group's interest as superior to all other interests.

animism

is the belief that recognises that animate spirits live in natural objects and operate in the world.

annexation

is the incorporation of one territory into another.

anomic suicides

are suicides that occur as a result of social unrest.

assimilation

is the integration of individuals into a dominant culture.

assimilationist minorities

are groups that seek to shed their old ways and integrate themselves into mainstream society.

atheism

is a rejection of the claims made by religions (such as the existence of an afterlife or deities), and is regarded as oppositional to theism.

attachment

is the social bond that refers to our relationship to others.
B

belief

refers to a person's conviction of truth.

bissu

are androgynous members of Bugis society who embody the perfect mixture of male and the female.

blended families

are families composed of children and some combination of biological parents.

bourgeoisie

refers to members of the capitalist class.

bureaucratisation

is the third stage of a movement, when it becomes a political force.

calabai

are anatomical males in Bugis society who adhere to some of the responsibilities of women.

calalai

are anatomical females in Bugis society who assume the characteristics of men.

campaigns

are ongoing efforts by groups in society to target specific authorities in an attempt to bring about change.

capital

the amount of economic, social and cultural capital an individual holds will impact their social position in a field.

capitalism

is an economic system in which individuals own the means of production and employ others to produce goods and services.

carrying capacity

is the number of a specific species that can exist in a given environment.

case studies

are investigations of one person or event in detail.

causal relationship

is a relationship in which one condition leads to a certain consequence.

causation

is the relationship between cause and effect.

central tendency

is the numbers in the middle of an array of numbers.

choice model

explains the income gap by analysing the kinds of jobs women choose.

church

a large, highly organised group of believers.

civil religion

is a binding force that holds society together through political and social issues.

class consciousness

is an understanding of one's position in the class system.

coalescence

is the second stage of a movement, when groups reach out to other groups and individuals to gain membership.

cohort

is a specific group of people used in a study.

collective consciousness

refers to the common beliefs and sentiments held by a group of individuals.

colonialism

is the imposition of control over a weak nation by a more powerful country.

colour-blind racism

is the idea that racism still exists in society in more subtle ways.

commitment

is the social bond that refers to our dedication to live a socially acceptable life.

comparative studies

use data from different sources in order to evaluate them against each other.

concepts

are abstract ideas that are impossible to measure.

conflict theory

is a theoretical framework that views society as an unequal system that brings about conflict and change.

conquest

is the domination over a group of people by a superior force.

contagion

is a rapid, irrational mode in which people don't think rationally or clearly.

containment theory

argues that criminals cannot resist the temptations that surround them.

content analysis

is a type of research in which the sociologist looks for common words or themes in newspapers, books or structured interviews.

control variables

are variables that are kept constant to accurately test the impact of an independent variable.

correlation

is an indication that one factor might be a cause for another factor.

cosmopolitanism

refers to a moral connection that binds all peoplesirrespective of race, ethnicity, gender or religion. It can be understood as a form of 'world citizenship'.

credentialism

is an emphasis on educational degrees in assessing skills and knowledge.

creolisation

is the cross-fertilisation of ideas, symbols, meanings and material culture (such as food, clothing and music) that occurs when different societies interact.

crime

is the violation of norms that have been written into law.

crime victim surveys

measure crimes against individuals or households with specific victims.

criminology

is the scientific study of crime, deviance and social policies that the criminal justice system applies. Criminological accounts of crime have been contrasted with media or political discourses of crime, which tend to be concerned with sensational aspects of crime.

cross-sectional studies

look at one event at a single point in time.

cults

are new religious movements led by charismatic leaders with few followers.

cultural industries

are the complex systems of production that create many forms of culture such as television shows, music and films.

cultural lag

happens when social and cultural changes occur at a slower pace than technological changes.

cultural relativism

consists of a deliberate effort to appreciate a group's way of life in its own context, without prejudice.

cultural shock

occurs when a person encounters a culture foreign to his or her own and has an emotional response to the differences between the cultures.

cultural transmission

is the process by which culture is passed on from generation to generation.

culture

is the language, beliefs, values, norms, behaviours and material objects that are important enough to pass on to future generations of a society.

dark figure of crime statistics

refers to crimes that are undetected, unreported and unrecorded.

de-peasantisation

is the movement of individuals, families and communities involved in subsistence agriculture from the farm to the regional towns and cities.

decline

is the final stage of a movement, when an organisation completes its goal or is seen as irrelevant.

delegated

means given or assigned.

demographic transition theory

is a projection that suggests people control their own fertility as they move from agrarian to industrial societies.

demography

is the study of population size and composition.

dependent variables

are the response to the manipulated variable.

deterrence

is a measure that prevents a person from doing something because of fear of the consequences.

detraditionalisation

is the erosion of traditional authority structures, practices and beliefs that have reproduced customs and cultural identity from early times.

deviance

is the violation of norms that a society agrees upon.

differential association theory

emphasises that criminal and deviant behaviour is learnt.

discretion

is the ability to make decisions.

discrimination

is the unfair treatment of people based on a prejudice.

disengagement theory

states that reduced interaction between older persons and others is unavoidable, mutual, and acceptable to both the individual and society.

dominant group

is the group that has the greatest power, but not necessarily the greatest numbers.

double consciousness

is the sense that a person must keep a foot in two worlds, one in the majority group's world and one in the minority group's world.

dramaturgy

is a theory of interaction in which all life is like acting.

ecological footprint

is a measure of the amount of productive land and water that is required to produce the resources that are consumed, and to absorb wastes, per head of population.

economic system

or economy, is the social system that helps a society organise what it produces, distributes and consumes.

education

is the formal system in which society passes its information and values from one generation to the next.

egoistic suicides

are suicides that result from a lack of solidarity, occurring among those who have few social connections, feel isolated and alone, and are more likely to fall into despair.

emergence

is the first stage of a movement, when people become aware of a problem and begin to notice that others feel the same way.

endogamy

is the practice of marrying within your social group.

environmental justice

is the impact of environmental factors on social classes.

environmental sociology

is the study of how the environment influences society, and vice versa.

ethics

is a system of values or principles that guide one's behaviour.

ethnic cleansing

refers to persecution through imprisonment, expulsion or murder of members of an ethnic minority by a majority to achieve ethnic homogeneity in majority-controlled territory.

ethnic enclaves

are neighbourhoods where people from similar cultures live together and assert cultural distinction from the dominant group.

ethnicity

is the classification of people who share a common cultural, linguistic or ancestral heritage.

ethnocentrism

occurs when a person uses his or her own culture to judge another culture.

ethnography

is a research method that aims to understand the social perspective and cultural values of a particular group by participating with or getting to know their activities in detail.

eugenics

is a social movement which sought to produce practical interventions to improve the genetic composition of human populations.

exchange mobility

is a concept suggesting that each social class contains a relatively fixed number of people.

exchange value

is the value of an object usually determined by symbolic association and is best typified by the higher value of certain 'brands' or identities.

exogamy

is the practice of marrying someone from a different social group.

extended family

is a household consisting of a nuclear family plus at least one additional relative.

false consciousness

is a person's lack of understanding of his or her position in society.

family

is two or more people who are related by blood, marriage, adoption or circumstance.

fatalistic suicides

are suicides that result from a lack of social control.

feminism

is the vast collection of social movements and theories about gender differences, proposing social equality for all people.

field refers

to a system of social positions structured according to power relations.

field research

is research conducted in a natural setting.

financialisation

describes the increasing role of financial motives, markets, actors and institutions in the operation of domestic and international economies.

folkways

are informal types of norms.

functional imperatives

are those conditions that must be met for a social system to operate.

functionalism

is a theoretical framework that defines society as a system of interrelated parts.

functions

are social factors that affect people in a society.

gender identity

is our perception of ourselves as male or female.

gender roles

are society's expectations of how males and females should act and think.

general deterrence

is a measure that ensures individuals won't commit a crime, because they see the negative consequences applied to others, and they fear experiencing these consequences.

generalisation

is the extent that what is learnt from a sample can be applied to the population from which the sample is taken.

generation

refers to age groupings that locate people historically.

generation gap

refers to the differences in experiences, values and assumptions of different generations which can then lead to tension between members of different generations.

genetic engineering (GE)

is the manipulation of the genetic makeup of cells or individual organisms via the insertion, removal or reworking of individual genes so that desirable traits can be expressed, reproduced and commercially developed.

genocide

is the attempt to destroy or exterminate a people based on their race and/or ethnicity.

geo-engineering

is the application of technology to change the environment of the earth and other celestial bodies.

gerontology

is the study of ageing and the elderly.

gestures

are symbols we make using our bodies, such as facial expressions, hand movements, eye contact and other types of body language.

glass ceiling

is an invisible barrier preventing women from reaching executive-level positions in the workplace.

global village

Marshall McLuhan's description of the sharing of ideas beyond the confines of communities and nations as a result of the growing ease of communication.

globalisation

can be considered a process which transforms social relationships across time and space by heightening the level of intensity, speed and impact of those relationships.

globo-enthusiast

is a person who considers that a more integrated world will be both more prosperous and more peaceful, and that nations which embrace globalisation can be liberated from undemocratic political regimes.

globo-sceptic

is a person who views globalisation as socially divisive, with economically and socially polarising effects that weaken democratic systems.

habitus

refers to a durable system of dispositions that determine how an individual acts.

Hawthorne effect

occurs when people behave differently because they know they are part of an experiment.

health

is a state of complete physical, mental and social well-being, and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity.

health care

is the care, services or supplies related to a person's health.

hegemony

is the power exerted by a dominant group.

homogamy

is marriage between people with similar backgrounds, such as religion, ethnicity, class or age.

horizontal mobility

refers to moving within the same status category.

human capital

is a person's combination of skills, knowledge, traits and personal attributes.

human capital model

assumes that men and women bring different natural skills to the workplace.

human exemptionalism

is the belief that humans are different from other species on earth.

hypothesis

involves a suggestion about how variables relate.

income

is the money received for work or through investments.

independent variables

are variables that are deliberately manipulated in an experiment.

indigenous superordination

is the subordination of an immigrant group to a dominant group.

institutional racism

defines the source for racism as social organisation and social structures.

intergenerational mobility

refers to the change that family members make from one social class to the next through generations.

intermarriage

is marriage between people of different religions.

intertextuality

describes references between various cultural forms.

intragenerational mobility

occurs when an individual changes social standing, especially in the workforce.

involuntary immigration

is the forced movement of people from one society to another.

involvement

is the social bond that refers to the level of activity in conventional things.

language

is a system of speech and/or written symbols used to convey meaning and communicate.

late modernity

is a slightly more moderate position than postmodernity, but describes similar social trends, including a greater sense of biographical uncertainty and heightened risk.

latent functions

are functions that lead to unforeseen or unexpected consequences.

life stages

are the different developmental phases people move through over a lifetime.

literature review

is a study of relevant academic articles and information.

longitudinal studies

include data from observations over time using a cohort.

lower middle class

is a social class that consists of those who have moderate incomes.

McDonaldisation

refers to the global spread of the principles and practices of the fast food industryin particular, those of McDonald's.

machismo

is overt and exaggerated displays of masculinity.

macro

means large-scale.

majority group

is the group that has the largest population in society and holds significant power and privilege.

Malthusian theorem

is a population projection that suggests the population will exceed the available food supply because populations grow at geometric rates, while food supplies grow at arithmetic rates.

manifest functions

are functions that lead to an expected consequence or outcome.

marginal poverty

is a state of poverty that occurs when a person lacks stable employment.

marital effects

are factors that make marriage work.

marriage

is the union of two people that is typically recognised by law or cultural norms.

material culture

consists of items within a culture that you can taste, touch and feel.

matriarchy

is a social system in which women are the main authority and hold power over men.

mean

is an average.

mechanical solidarity

refers to the state of community bonding in traditional societies in which people share beliefs and values and perform common activities.

median

refers to the midpoint in a distribution of numbers.

medicalisation

is the idea that the medical community is at the centre of many aspects of Australian society.

Medicare

is a government-run social insurance program that provides health coverage for Australians.

meritocracy argument

states that those who get ahead do so based on their own merit.

micro

means small-scale.

migrant superordination

is the conquest of a native population by a more powerful group.

militant minorities

are groups that seek to overthrow the existing system because they see it as unjust.

minority group

is a group that has a smaller population and less power than the majority group.

mode

refers to the most common value in a distribution of numbers.

modernity

is a historical epoch characterised by industrialisation, secularisation and the rise of capitalism.

monogamy

is the practice of being married to one person at a time.

monotheism

is the belief that there is only one god.

mores

are norms that represent a community's most important values.

multiculturalism

is a concept supporting the inherent value of different cultures.

national crime statistics

are official police statistics of reported crimes, collated by the Australian Bureau of Statistics.

negative correlation

occurs when the variables move in opposite directions.

neo-liberalism

is an approach to economic and social policy which favours deregulation, an open market and limited government.

neo-tribe

describes an ephemeral, fluid configuration of styles and tastes.

new racism

explains differences in human populations with reference to essential cultural traits, rather than biology.

non-material culture

consists of the non-physical products of society, including our symbols, values, rules and sanctions.

normative relativism

bases the evaluation of a society on that society's own norms.

norms

are rules developed for appropriate behaviour based on specific values that are conditional.

nuclear family

is a household consisting of a husband, wife and children.

obesity

is an unhealthy accumulation of body fat.

objectivity

is the ability to conduct research without allowing personal biases or prejudices to influence you.

'old old'

is a cohort that consists of people over the age of 75.

operationalising

is turning abstract ideas into something measurable.

organic solidarity

occurs when people live in a society with a diverse division of labour.

organisation of believers

is a group that ensures the prosperity and effectiveness of the religious experience.

oversampling

is the process of taking a bigger sample if the group you wish to study makes up a small percentage of the whole population.

paradigms

are theoretical frameworks through which sociologists study the social world.

participant observation

is a type of field research in which the researcher poses as a person who is normally in the research setting.

patriarchy

is a social system in which the father serves as head of the family, and men have authority over women and children.

patriarchy model

assumes that we have a male-dominated society that doesn't allow women to hold upper-tier jobs.

philosophies of life

are ways of life that focus on a set of ethical, moral or philosophical principles.

pluralistic minorities

are groups that enter into an area voluntarily but seek to maintain their own culture while also integrating into the dominant group.

polyandry

is the practice of a woman marrying two or more men.

polygamy

is the practice of having more than one spouse at a time.

polygyny

is the practice of a man marrying two or more women.

polytheism

is the belief in multiple gods and demigods.

populations

are target groups from which researchers want to get information.

positive correlation

includes two variables that move in a parallel direction.

postdenominationalism

is a recent trend that stretches religious boundaries.

postmodernism

is the corresponding aesthetic style and movement of postmodernity, characterised by irony, pastiche and intertextuality. Postmodernism is also, importantly, a critical tradition that brings into question the modernist belief in meta-narratives or overarching truths.

postmodernity

is a historical epoch (according to some) that we are currently occupying, characterised by individualisation, increased investment in media and new technologies, and a distrust of authorities and an overarching sense of truth.

power

is the ability to carry out your will and impose it on others.

power elite

is a small group of people who hold immense power.

prejudice

refers to negative attitudes about an entire category of people.

prestige

is the level of esteem associated with one's status and social standing.

primary deviance

is the initial deviant act itself.

profane

means related or devoted to that which is not sacred or biblical.

progressive

means favouring or promoting change.

proletariat

refers to members of the poor working class.

protestant ethic

is a robust work ethic where the rewards of one's labour are reinvested into that labour, with hard work leading to personal salvation.

qualitative data

include words, pictures, photos, or any other type of information that comes to the researcher in a non-numeric form.

quantitative data

are data based on numbers.

race

is the division of people based on certain physical characteristics, especially skin colour.

racism

is discrimination based on a person's race.

random sample

is a group of subjects arbitrarily chosen from a defined population.

recidivism

is repetitious criminal activity. Synonymous with terms such as repeat offending and reoffending.

redemptive social movements

focus on specific individuals, but the amount of change sought is radical, rather than limited.

reformative social movements

seek to change a society's thoughts and actions, but only in a limited way.

regressive

means seeking to stop change.

reintegrative shaming

is an effort to bring an offender back into their community after punishment.

relative poverty

is a state of poverty that occurs when we compare ourselves to those around us.

reliable

means able to be trusted.

religion

is a unified system of beliefs and practices, relative to sacred things; that is to say, things set apart and forbiddenbeliefs and practices which unite into one single moral community called a Church, all those who adhere to them.

repertoires

are actions used to promote interest and involvement within the movement.

research design

refers to the process used to find information.

research methods

are the scientific procedures that sociologists use to conduct research and develop knowledge about a particular topic.

residual poverty

is chronic and multigenerational poverty.

revolutionary social movement

, sometimes called the transformative social movement, seeks to change the thoughts and actions of all society in a radical fashion.

rituals

are established patterns of behaviour closely associated with experience of the sacred.

sacred

means connected to God or dedicated to a religious purpose.

sample

is a subset of a population.

sanctions

are prizes or punishments received when you either abide by a norm or violate it.

sandwiched generation

is the generation that takes care both of its children and its elderly parents.

Sapir-Whorf hypothesis

is a hypothesis, first advanced by Edward Sapir in 1929 and subsequently developed by Benjamin Whorf, that the structure of language determines a native speaker's perception and categorisation of experience.

scapegoat

means making an unfair accusation against a person or group as the cause of a problem.

secessionist minorities

are groups that voluntarily separate themselves from the dominant group and view the dominant group with disdain, believing that it will corrupt the group's belief system.

secondary data

are data that others have already collected and published.

secondary data analysis

is the process of using and analysing data that others have collected.

secondary deviance

refers to the psychological reorientation that occurs when the system catches a person and labels him or her as a deviant.

sects

are religious groups that have enough members to sustain themselves and go against society's norms.

secularisation

is the overall decline in the importance and power of religion in people's lives.

segregation

is where ethnic groups receive differential treatment and are provided with separate services.

selection effects

are the likelihood that a non-representative sample of the population may lead to inaccurate results.

self

refers to a person's identity and what makes that person different from others.

sex

is the biological makeup of a male or female.

sexism

is the belief that one sex is superior to the other.

shaming

is a deliberate effort to attach a negative meaning to a behaviour.

sick role

is the expected behaviours and responsibilities appropriate for someone who is ill.

simple supernaturalism

is the belief in a variety of supernatural forces that affect and influence people's lives.

social control

refers to the social mechanisms that regulate a person's actions.

social Darwinism

is a notion that suggests strong societies survive and weak ones become extinct.

social dynamics

are the change in existing structural elements of society.

social epidemiology

is the study of the distribution of diseases and health throughout a society's population.

social institutions

are structures that provide for patterned relationships.

social laws

are statements of fact that are unchanging under given conditions and can be used as ground rules for any kind of society.

social mobility

is the ability to change social classes.

social movements

are activities that support or protest social issues organised by nongovernmental organisations.

social statics

are the existing structural elements of society.

social stratification

is the ranking of people and the rewards they receive based on objective criteria, often including wealth, power and/or prestige.

social structures

are patterns of relationships that endure from one generation to the next.

socialisation

is the process that teaches the norms, values and other aspects of a culture to new group members.

socialism

is the economic system by which resources and the means of production are owned collectively by the citizens.

sociological imagination

is the ability to look beyond the individual as the cause of success and failure, and to see how one's society influences the outcome.

sociology

is a science guided by the basic understanding that 'the social matters: our lives are affected, not only by our individual characteristics but by our place in the social world'.

solidarity

refers to the level of connectedness and integration a person feels to others in the environment.

specific deterrence

is a measure that changes the attitude of individuals who have already violated the law and have been punished, by causing them never to commit a crime again.

spurious

, or non-genuine correlation, occurs when two variables appear to be related, but actually have a different cause.

stereotypes

are simplified perceptions people have of an entire group that are usually based on false assumptions.

stigmatised shame

is a permanent label given to an offender, which could actually increase the chances of reoffending because the guilty person is forever labelled.

street crime

refers to publicly visible crimes and acts popularly defined as criminal, such as assault, burglary and rape. In contrast, less visible crimes, such as domestic violence, white-collar or environmental crime, are not associated with street crime.

structural mobility

occurs when social changes affect large numbers of people.

subculture

is a sociological term, first used in the study of youth deviance and later in the study of youth style and taste.

survey

is an investigation of the opinions or experience of a group of people by asking them questions.

symbolic interactionism

is a theoretical framework that focuses on how people interact with others in their everyday lives.

symbols

represent, suggest or stand for something else.

system of beliefs

relates sacred objects to religious rituals, and defines and protects the sacred from the profane.

taboos

are acts that are socially unacceptable.

teacher expectancy effect

is the impact of a teacher's expectations on a student's performance.

technopoly

is a society that values technological change for its own sake.

texts

are aspects of culture that have an influence on our understanding of the world.

theism

is the belief in a god or gods.

theocracy

is a state religion that is formed when government and religion both work together to shape society.

theory

is a comprehensive and systematic explanation of events that lead to testable predictions.

thriftiness

is extreme unwillingness to use a large number of resources.

totemism

is the practice of honouring a totem or a sacred object.

transitional poverty

is a temporary state of poverty that occurs when someone loses a job for a short time.

transnational corporations

are those which operate in at least two countries and have the interests of their company, rather than the interests of their home country, at heart.

triangulation

is the process of using multiple approaches to study a phenomenon.

underclass

is a social class living in disadvantaged neighbourhoods that are characterised by four components: poverty, family disruption, male unemployment, and lack of individuals in high-status occupations.

upper middle class

is a social class that consists of high-income members of society who are well educated but don't belong to the elite super-wealthy class.

upper

or elite class, is a social class that is very small in number and holds significant wealth.

use value

is the value of an object based on what it does.

validity

assures that you are actually measuring the thing you set out to measure in the first place.

value clusters

are two or more values that support each other.

value conflict

occurs when two or more values are at odds.

value pairs

help us define values, usually in terms of opposites.

values

are a part of a society's non-material culture that represent cultural standards by which we determine what is good, bad, right or wrong.

vertical mobility

refers to moving from one social status to another.

voluntary immigration

is the willing movement of people from one society to another.

wealth

is all of your material possessions, including income.

working class

is a social class generally made up of people with high school certificates and lower levels of education.

world system theory

Wallerstein's theory of the world in three parts: a core (the conquering, rich, countries), semi-periphery and the periphery (the conquered, poorer, nations).

WUNC

refers to the members of a movement who want to show the public the worthiness, unity, numbers and commitments of their movement.

xenocentrism

is when we perceive other groups or societies as superior to our own.

xenophobia

refers to fear and hostility towards people who are from other countries and cultures.

young old

is a cohort that consists of people between the ages of 65 and 75.

youth

is a sociological life stage and the principal transitional phase before adulthood.
